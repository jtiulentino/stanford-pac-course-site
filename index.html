MED 277

AI-Assisted Health Care 1-4 Units

Stanford University Fall Quarter 2018

AI has been advancing quickly, with its impact everywhere. In healthcare, innovation in AI could help transforming of our healthcare system.

This course offers a diverse set of research projects focusing on cutting-edge computer vision and machine learning technologies to solve some of healthcare's most important problems. The teaching team and teaching assistants will work closely with students on research projects in this area. Research projects include Care for Senior at Senior Home, Surgical Quality Analysis, AI Assisted Parenting, Burn Analysis & Assessment and more. AI areas include Video Understanding, Image Classification, Objected Detection, Segmentation, Action Recognition, Deep Learning, Reinforcement Learning, HCI, and more.

The course is open to students in both the School of Medicine and the School of Engineering.

Projects

Hand Hygiene Intervention
Hand Hygiene Intervention in Hospitals
On average, 1 in 25 patients get hospital-acquired infections leading to 99,000 deaths and billions of dollars per year in the U.S. Yet, healthcare workers are only washing their hands one-third to one-half the times they should be. This project will design a just-in-time, computer vision-based hospital hand hygiene intervention to remind healthcare workers to wash their hands. The project aims to: 1) improve hand hygiene compliance and 2) prevent hospital-acquired infections. Students will conduct needfinding analyses of healthcare workers, scope and define the specific research question to tackle, brainstorm intervention ideas, build prototypes of designs, and install and test designs in the hospital environment. Deep learning and design thinking experience is encouraged of students who apply. Projects will target relevant publication venues throughout the year.
Gait Analysis
Gait and Mobility Analysis for Senior Home
Mobility level is fundamental to active aging and is closely related to health status and quality of life. Although there is widespread acceptance regarding the mobility level of senior people, few attempts have been successful in accurately detect the mobility level using vision-based approach. In this project, we are interested in the analysis of seniorsâ€™ manner of walking. More specifically, we focus on the detection of their mobility level in terms of the number of steps taken or in terms of the absolute moving distance. We collected two weeks of continuous video data from two senior home sites, and the videos come with two modalities: depth and thermal. We will tackle this problem by using the cutting-edge deep learning algorithms for video understanding and human pose estimation.
Privacy-Preserving ML
Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning
Patient and data privacy is especially important for healthcare settings, and there is a lot of potential research in privacy-preserving machine learning. The goal of this project is to leverage techniques including homomorphic encryption and differential privacy on deep learning models with various medical datasets. Students will learn about fundamental deep learning models including CNNs and LSTMs and common machine learning frameworks including Pytorch and Tensorflow, as well as cryptographic and statistical concepts used in privacy preservation. Over the year, members of the project will have the opportunity to participate in both applied-ML engineering and theoretical research. Systems and prior machine learning experience is highly recommended.
Long-Range Hand Tracking
Long-Range Hand Tracking in Hospitals
Hands are the most important body part for interacting with the world. As a result, hand tracking is extremely important for human-computer interaction in virtual reality, robotics, and video analysis. However, hands also spread the most germs. Hospital acquired infections kill 99,000 people per year. If we can accurately track hands, we hope it can reduce these infections. This project aims to track hands and fingers of multiple people at long distances (10 ft+) using 3D video data from a real hospital. Technical solutions will span deep learning, computer graphics, graph matching, and pose estimation. Healthcare applications include infection control, patient safety monitoring, and hospital activity recognition.
Rare Events Synthesis
Rare Events Synthesis and Detection for Senior Home
For senior healthcare, detecting fall events is a very important task, since fall events often lead to disastrous outcomes. According to the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, one in four Americans aged 65+ falls each year, and an older adult dies from a fall every 19 minutes. From the computer vision perspective, fall detection is challenging because real-world fall incidents are rare and usually not enough to train a machine learning model. Therefore, our goal is to synthesize rare event data to train a model that achieves better performance in identifying rare events. The first part of the project will be using computer graphics to generate data. The second part of the project will be synthesizing more realistic data, either by refining the CG-generated data to be more realistic, or by directly generating realistic data from latent random variables.
Mobility Level Profile
Mobility Level Profile for ICU
The early mobilization of patients in the intensive care unit (ICU) has received considerable attention in clinical and scientific literature over the past several years. A wide range of published reports has attempted to study the effects of mobilization and physical therapy on multiple factors including patient safety, ambulation capacity, muscle strength, functional outcomes such as activities of daily living, duration of mechanical ventilation, ICU length of stay, hospital length of stay, and mortality. The ICU team is working towards a system that uses vision-based methods to determine the mobility level of ICU patients, which can support a downstream clinical study on the correlation between patients' mobility level and post-ICU outcomes.
